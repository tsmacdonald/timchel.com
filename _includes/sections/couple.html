Tim + Rachel

Rachel's picture (on the family prayer card) hung on Tim's parents' refrigerator for a decade and was mostly ignored; we never expected to meet. But we did meet, at a tiny Anglican churchplant in Chicago. We slowly went from acquaintances to church friends to real friends, but we never expected to date—even if Tim spent six years hoping to. Much to everyone's surprise (most of all ours) the hope became reality, after much hesitation. And on Leap Day 2020 an even bigger hope came true, with no hesitation:

"I was wondering…if you would marry me?"

"Of course!"

See all of our engagement photos (taken by Hello Rose Photography) here.

For everything there is a season, and a time for every matter under heaven: …a time to embrace, and a time to refrain from embracing (Ecc. 3.1, 5b)

When Tim proposed on Leap Day 2020, we both eagerly envisioned celebrating our wedding in Chicago—our home of the past six years—surrounded by our friends and family from near and far.

As the pandemic unfolded, we soon realized such a large gathering would not be advisable for everyone's health and safety, and that gatherings of any sort would likely be prohibited in Illinois. Our wedding has now been moved to Sewickley, PA (where Tim grew up), where we will be married in the presence of our immediate families.

We will miss having you all there, but we will have a live stream set up so that you can watch Tim's dad marry us and catch Rachel's dad preaching the homily! Be on the lookout for invitations to our Chicago reception next year.

We covet your prayers as we enter into marriage in the midst of this season.
